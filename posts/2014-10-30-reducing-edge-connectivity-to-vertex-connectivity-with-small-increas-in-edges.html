Reducing edge connectivity to vertex connectivity with small increase in edges


Problem1

Let G=(V,E)G=(V,E)G=(V,E) be a graph, we are interested in finding a graph G′=(V′,E′)G'=(V',E')G′=(V′,E′), such that GGG is a minor of G′G'G′. We want a partition PPP of V′V'V′ and a bijection f:P→Vf:P\to Vf:P→V, such that for any s∈Ss\in Ss∈S, t∈Tt\in Tt∈T and S,T∈PS,T\in PS,T∈P, we have κG′(s,t)=λG(f(S),f(T))\kappa_{G'}(s,t) = \lambda_{G}(f(S),f(T))κG′​(s,t)=λG​(f(S),f(T)).

Although there is rarely a reason to reduce edge connectivity computation to vertex connectivity computation, because vertex connectivity is much harder in almost every way. It is still a interesting exercise.

The naive method: For each vertex vvv, expand it into a clique of size deg(v)deg(v)deg(v), and find a bijective label between new vertex in the clique with the edges incident to vvv. Connect all the vertices with the same label. Thus in the worst case, we can loosely bound the edges in G′G'G′ to be ∑vdeg2(v)≤∑v(m/n)2=m2/n=O(nm)\sum_{v} deg^2(v)\leq \sum_{v} (m/n)^2 = m^2/n = O(nm)∑v​deg2(v)≤∑v​(m/n)2=m2/n=O(nm).

There is another way with the edge blow up to be O(nm)O(nm)O(nm) [1].

However, we want to blow up the number of edges by at most O(m)O(m)O(m).

Definition2

A nnn-connector is a graph with two disjoint set of vertices III (inputs) and OOO (outputs) such that ∣I∣=∣O∣=n|I|=|O|=n∣I∣=∣O∣=n, and for any bijection f:I→Of:I\to Of:I→O, there exist vertex disjoint paths connecting vvv and f(v)f(v)f(v) simultaneously.

It is known a nnn-connector exists with O(n)O(n)O(n) edges, because there is a even stronger notion of nnn-nonblocking graph[2].

nnn-connector seems to be what we need here, but it forces us to have input and output vertices. Thus we can define the following.

Definition3

A nnn-symmetric-connector is a graph with a set of nnn vertices TTT, such that for any fff a bijection between two disjoint subsets of TTT, there exist vertex disjoint paths connecting vvv and f(v)f(v)f(v) simultaneously.

Let GGG be a nnn-connected with inputs III and outputs OOO, we construct a nnn-symmetric connector by make a copy of OOO as O′O'O′, maintaining the edges of the copies too. Then we pick fff any bijection between III and OOO, and contract vvv and f(v)f(v)f(v) into one vertex. The contracted vertex will be the set TTT, and the new graph is a nnn-symmetric-connector.
