Proof that binomial coefficients are integers


Eagle Fantasy asked how to prove that binomial coefficients are integers without induction or counting. He seek a purely number theoretical proof.

One common way to prove a number is a integer is to show it has a integer factorization. Thus one try to demonstrate that (nk){n \choose k}(kn​) has such factorization.

Let's start by figure out what is the factorization of n!n!n!. Consider any prime ppp. Certainly all numbers from 111 to nnn that is divisible by ppp contribute to the exponent by 111, numbers that divisible by p2p^2p2 contribute to the exponent by 111, as we already counted one of the ppp factor already.
