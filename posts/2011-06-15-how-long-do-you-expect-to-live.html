How long do you expect to live?


While discussing conditional probability, someone said the following:

Problem1

The expected life expectancy of some country is 70, and there exist people who die at every age before 70. What is the expected life expectancy for a 60 year old?

Most people would answer 10. However, he continuous:

It could be 10, but for many distributions, it's likely more than that. You can convince yourself by thinking about the expected life expectancy for a 80 year old.

The quote above would follow directly from the proof of the following theorem:

Theorem2

For any real random variable XXX, if Pr⁡(X≥a)>0\Pr(X\geq a)>0Pr(X≥a)>0, E[X∣X≥a]≥E[X]E[X|X\geq a] \geq E[X]E[X∣X≥a]≥E[X].

Proof

Let c=Pr⁡(X≤a)c = \Pr(X\leq a)c=Pr(X≤a) E[X]=∫−∞∞xPr⁡(X=x)dx=∫−∞axPr⁡(X=x)dx+∫a∞xPr⁡(X=x)dx=∫−∞∞xPr⁡(X=x∣X≤a)Pr⁡(X≤a)dx+∫−∞∞xPr⁡(X=x∣X≥a)Pr⁡(X≥a)dx=c∫−∞∞xPr⁡(X=x∣X≤a)dx+(1−c)∫−∞∞xPr⁡(X=x∣X≥a)dx=cE[X∣X≤a]+(1−c)E[X∣X≥a]\displaystyle \begin{aligned} E[X] &= \int_{-\infty}^\infty x \Pr(X=x) dx\\ &=\int_{-\infty}^a x \Pr(X=x) dx + \int_a^\infty x \Pr(X=x) dx\\ &=\int_{-\infty}^\infty x \Pr(X=x|X\leq a)\Pr(X\leq a) dx + \int_{-\infty}^\infty x \Pr(X=x|X\geq a)\Pr(X\geq a) dx\\ &=c\int_{-\infty}^\infty x \Pr(X=x|X\leq a) dx + (1-c)\int_{-\infty}^\infty x \Pr(X=x|X\geq a) dx \\ &=cE[X|X\leq a] + (1-c)E[X|X\geq a] \\ \end{aligned}E[X]​=∫−∞∞​xPr(X=x)dx=∫−∞a​xPr(X=x)dx+∫a∞​xPr(X=x)dx=∫−∞∞​xPr(X=x∣X≤a)Pr(X≤a)dx+∫−∞∞​xPr(X=x∣X≥a)Pr(X≥a)dx=c∫−∞∞​xPr(X=x∣X≤a)dx+(1−c)∫−∞∞​xPr(X=x∣X≥a)dx=cE[X∣X≤a]+(1−c)E[X∣X≥a]​

If a=λb+(1−λ)ca = \lambda b + (1-\lambda) ca=λb+(1−λ)c, where λ∈[0,1]\lambda \in [0,1]λ∈[0,1], then a≤max⁡(b,c)a \leq \max(b,c)a≤max(b,c). Because E[X∣X≤a]≤a≤E[X∣X≥a]E[X|X\leq a]\leq a \leq E[X|X\geq a]E[X∣X≤a]≤a≤E[X∣X≥a], E[X]≤E[X∣X≥a]E[X] \leq E[X|X\geq a]E[X]≤E[X∣X≥a].

In fact, one can easily modify the above proof and prove the next theorem:

Theorem3

For any real random variable XXX, if x≥yx\geq yx≥y and Pr⁡(X≥x)>0\Pr(X\geq x)>0Pr(X≥x)>0, then E[X∣X≥x]≥E[X∣X≥y]E[X|X\geq x] \geq E[X|X\geq y]E[X∣X≥x]≥E[X∣X≥y].

A heuristics conclusion: The longer you lived, you expect to live longer.
