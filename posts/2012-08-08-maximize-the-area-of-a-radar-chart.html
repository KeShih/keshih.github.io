Maximize the area of a radar chart


People use radar charts to present their ability in different skills.

Like all self presentations, it need to make the person "look good". A chart with larger area looks better than the ones with less area. The remaining of the article take about how to find a radar chart with maximum area.

In the image above, the left side has less area than the right.

Given the sequence of data (a1,c1),…,(an,cn)(a_1,c_1),\ldots,(a_n,c_n)(a1​,c1​),…,(an​,cn​), where aia_iai​ is the value, a positive number, cic_ici​ is a coordinate(in the image, the coordinates would be "combinatorics", "algorithms", etc.). Theorem2

Let a1,…,ana_1,\ldots,a_na1​,…,an​ be a sequence of positive numbers, such that a1≤…≤ana_1 \leq \ldots \leq a_na1​≤…≤an​.

Let π\piπ be the following:

Proof

Proof by induction.

Base case: Base case for n=1,2n=1,2n=1,2 are trivial.

Inductive Step: Consider it is true for n−1n-1n−1, we want to show it is true for nnn.

Consider we already obtained a sequence from a1a_1a1​ to an−1a_{n-1}an−1​, and we want to insert ana_nan​ somewhere. Clearly ana_nan​ need to be placed in a position that maximizes its contribution. If we can insert it between an−1a_{n-1}an−1​ and an−2a_{n-2}an−2​, then we get a contribution of an−1an+an−2an−an−1an−2a_{n-1}a_n+a_{n-2}a_n-a_{n-1}a_{n-2}an−1​an​+an−2​an​−an−1​an−2​. By the lemma, it will give us the maximum contribution.

If we can get the maximum contribution (over all possible configurations) to a maximum configuration for a1a_1a1​ to an−1a_{n-1}an−1​, then we are done. The inductive hypothesis show the maximum configuration have adjacent an−1a_{n-1}an−1​ and an−2a_{n-2}an−2​, therefore this shows the maximum configuration is where we insert ana_nan​ in between an−1a_{n-1}an−1​ and an−2a_{n-2}an−2​ in the previous maximum configuration.

This implies a simple O(nlog⁡n)O(n \log n)O(nlogn) algorithm. Find the permutation that sort the input sequence by value, then composes it with permutation π\piπ.

Actually this problem is a special case of the TSP problem on a product matrix. A matrix is called a product matrix if Mi,j=aibjM_{i,j} = a_ib_jMi,j​=ai​bj​ for vectors (a1,…,an)(a_1,\ldots,a_n)(a1​,…,an​) and (b1,…,bn)(b_1,\ldots,b_n)(b1​,…,bn​). It is solvable in polynomial time if MMM is a symmetric matrix [1].
