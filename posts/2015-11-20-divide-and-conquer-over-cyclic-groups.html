Divide and conquer over cyclic groups


Recently we found a divide and conquer algorithm over Zm​. We have an efficient algorithm for the case where all the elements S are in Zm∗​, the set of units.

Our divide and conquer algorithm basically partition the numbers by throwing them into different subgroups. Assume m has distinct prime factors p1​<…<pk​, and for simplicity, define p0​=1. We partition S to S0​,…,Sk​, where Si​ contain all the elements that’s divisible by pi​ but not pj​ for any j>i. We recursively apply our algorithm to each Si​/pi​ in Zm/pi​​ and combine the solution.

This gives us a recurrence relation, and the crucial part of the recurrence involves the following function.
